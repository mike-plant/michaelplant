**STREETLIGHT PAGE SWEEP (PRINT THIS)**
=======================================

1\. **Streetlight (Main Page)**
-------------------------------

**Purpose:** Orientation + framing**Job:**

*   Name the problem
*   Define what Streetlight is
*   Define who it’s for
*   Exit cleanly

**Must do:**

*   High-level only
*   Establish credibility through framing, not explanation
*   Overlap cleanly with your broader life/work

**Must NOT do:**

*   Teach Google
*   Explain process in detail
*   Mention pricing specifics
*   Reference site structure

**Reader should think:**

> “I understand what this is and whether it applies to me.”

2\. **What Google Looks For**
-----------------------------

**Purpose:** Authority + explanation**Job:**

*   Show you understand how local search _actually_ works
*   Translate abstract signals into real-world behavior
*   Build trust through judgment and ordering

**Must do:**

*   Explain relevance & prominence in plain language
*   Include real-world examples and symptoms
*   Emphasize order of operations

**Must NOT do:**

*   Sell
*   Talk about pricing
*   Describe your service mechanics

**Reader should think:**

> “This person knows what they’re talking about.”

3\. **Why Apple Maps & Yelp Still Matter**
------------------------------------------

**Purpose:** Secondary authority + blind-spot correction**Job:**

*   Explain quiet friction and inconsistency
*   Name problems people _feel_ but can’t diagnose
*   Support the Google page without repeating it

**Must do:**

*   Explain cross-platform trust
*   Name subtle damage
*   Offer a simple mental model (one source of truth)

**Must NOT do:**

*   Re-explain Google ranking
*   Pitch services
*   Get tactical

**Reader should think:**

> “Oh — that explains why things feel off.”

4\. **Streetlight Audit (Audit + Cleanup Page)**
------------------------------------------------

**Purpose:** Reassurance + optionality**Job:**

*   Make the work feel safe, bounded, and understandable
*   Show exactly what you look at
*   Present DIY vs DFY as _the same truth_
    

**Must do:**

*   Be transparent
*   Name real blockers
*   Emphasize clarity and order

**Must NOT do:**

*   Oversell
*   Repeat Google theory
*   Turn into a pricing page

**Reader should think:**

> “This feels reasonable. I can copy this or hand it off.”

5\. **Pricing**
---------------

**Purpose:** Decision support**Job:**

*   State prices clearly
*   Clarify what’s included
*   Catch ready buyers without pressure
*   Light directional cues only

**Must do:**

*   Assume some readers skipped context
*   Point gently to deeper pages if needed
*   Stay conversational

**Must NOT do:**

*   Teach the system
*   Re-justify the work
*   Repeat philosophy

**Reader should think:**

> “I know what this costs and what happens if I say yes.”

6\. **Websites Still Matter (Optional / Supporting Page)**
----------------------------------------------------------

**Purpose:** Systems thinking + trust reinforcement**Job:**

*   Reframe the role of websites in local discovery
*   Connect listings ↔ website consistency
*   Show you think end-to-end

**Must do:**

*   Support Streetlight indirectly
*   Use real examples (like Wandering Lantern)

**Must NOT do:**

*   Become a sales page
*   Compete with the main Streetlight page

**Reader should think:**

> “This person understands the whole system, not just listings.”

**ONE-SENTENCE RULE (KEEP THIS AT THE TOP OF YOUR NOTES)**
==========================================================

> If a paragraph could live on more than one page, it probably doesn’t belong on either.

Each page should feel **slightly incomplete on purpose**, because the system as a whole is complete.
